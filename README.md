## üìñ Overview

The Sales & Profit Dashboard is a dynamic Excel-based analytics tool designed to visualize key business metrics ‚Äî including sales performance, profitability, and customer distribution ‚Äî across multiple dimensions such as Category, Region, Year, and Month. This is a hybrid of BI design, automation scripting, and user-experience engineering. 

The project applies Descriptive Analytics and KPI Theory to summarize and measure business performance, enhanced through Data Visualization principles for effective communication. Automation and HCI theories are incorporated through VBA macros, enabling user interactivity and efficiency. Data Quality frameworks such as ETL and integrity validation ensure the accuracy of insights. Together, these theories support a data-driven decision-making process that enhances business understanding and operational performance.

## üß© Key Features

- Interactive Slicers: Filter data by Category, Year, and Month to explore trends dynamically.
- KPIs at a Glance: Real-time summary of Total Sales and Total Profit.
- Visual Insights:
  1. Profit by Year bar chart for tracking growth over time.
  2. Sales by Category funnel chart to highlight top-performing product lines.
  3. Customer Count donut chart to show customer distribution by year.
  4. Sales by State filled map for regional analysis.
  5. Sales by Month bar chart revealing seasonality trends.
  6. Top Five Customers horizontal bar chart spotlighting major clients.
  7. Consistent Design: Unified color scheme, clean layout, and custom icons for professional presentation.

----

## üìà Key Insights
- **Top Performing Categories:** Technology consistently achieved the highest profit margins.  
- **Regional Trends:** Certain states had high sales but low profitability ‚Äî indicating possible pricing or cost challenges.  
- **Product Performance:** A few sub-categories contributed disproportionately to overall profit (Pareto 80/20 insight).  
- **Seasonal Trends:** Q4 showed peak sales, likely due to promotional periods and year-end demand.
